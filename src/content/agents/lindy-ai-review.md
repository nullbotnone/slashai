## What Is Lindy AI?

Lindy is an AI operations agent that connects to your email, calendar, CRM, and other tools, then handles repetitive tasks autonomously. Unlike chatbots that wait for you to ask questions, Lindy operates on triggers — new email arrives, new lead fills out a form, meeting ends — and takes action based on rules you set.

Think of it as a virtual assistant that costs $49/mo instead of $2,000/mo.

## The Good

### Genuinely Autonomous
This is what separates Lindy from Zapier or Make.com. You don't build workflows step-by-step. You tell Lindy: "When a new lead emails us, qualify them based on these criteria, update HubSpot, and draft a personalized reply." Lindy figures out the steps.

After setup, we went 3 days without checking Lindy. It handled 47 emails, scheduled 3 meetings, and updated 8 leads — all correctly.

### Email Triage Is Excellent
Lindy categorizes emails by urgency and type with about 90% accuracy. High-priority client emails got flagged immediately. Newsletters and spam got filtered. Action items got extracted and added to our task list.

The draft replies are surprisingly good — they match your tone after learning from 10-20 of your past emails.

### 3,000+ Integrations
Lindy connects to everything: Gmail, Outlook, HubSpot, Salesforce, Slack, Zoom, Google Calendar, Stripe, Notion, and thousands more via native integrations and Zapier.

### Setup Wizard Is Smart
Instead of blank configuration screens, Lindy walks you through setup with questions: "What do you want me to do with new leads?" "How should I prioritize emails?" "Who gets meetings automatically scheduled?" Setup took about 90 minutes.

---

## The Bad

### $49/mo Is Steep for Early Solopreneurs
If you're making $1,000/mo, spending $49 on an AI agent feels like a lot. Lindy makes sense once you're at $3K+/mo and admin is genuinely eating into billable hours.

### Credit System Is Confusing
You never quite know how many credits an action will use. A simple email read might use 1 credit; a complex qualification workflow might use 50. Hard to predict monthly usage.

### Occasional Over-Automation
In week 2, Lindy sent a follow-up email to a client who had already responded — just on a different thread. Embarrassing but not catastrophic. You need to monitor it, especially in the first month.

### Learning Curve for Complex Workflows
Simple automations (triage email, update CRM) are easy. Multi-step workflows (qualify lead → score → route to correct pipeline → send sequence) take trial and error to get right.
