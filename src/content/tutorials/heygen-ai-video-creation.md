Click **Create Video** → **Text to Video** to open the editor.

---

## Step 3: Write Your Script

The script editor is a simple text box. Type or paste your script — HeyGen will have your avatar speak every word.

**Tips for good AI video scripts:**

- **Write conversationally.** Read it out loud. If it sounds stiff, rewrite it.
- **Keep paragraphs short.** One to two sentences per paragraph works best.
- **Use punctuation for pacing.** Commas and periods control pauses naturally.
- **Aim for 150–200 words per minute** of video.

**Example script for a product intro:**

```
Hey there. If you're a freelancer drowning in admin work, this might be the tool you've been looking for.

I've been using InvoiceFlow for three months, and here's what changed.

Instead of spending two hours every Friday on invoices, I now spend ten minutes. The AI categorizes expenses, generates professional invoices, and sends payment reminders automatically.

My clients pay faster, I stress less, and I actually get to enjoy my Fridays again.

Check the link below to start your free trial.
```

Write your own script, or use this as a template and swap in your own product or service.

## Step 4: Choose Your Avatar

Click the avatar panel on the left side of the editor. You have three options:

### Public Avatars
Browse the library of pre-made avatars. Filter by style (realistic, casual, professional), age, gender, and ethnicity. Click any avatar to preview how it looks.

**Tip:** Match the avatar to your brand tone. A casual tech product? Pick someone relaxed. B2B consulting? Go with a professional look.

### Photo Avatar
Upload a single portrait photo and HeyGen animates it — facial expressions, lip-sync, and gestures. Great for creating a branded character or using your own headshot without recording video.

### Digital Twin (Creator plan)
Record a short video of yourself, and HeyGen creates a realistic digital clone. This is the most personal option — your exact look and voice, without ever hitting record again.

For your first video, pick a **Public Avatar**. It's fast, requires zero setup, and looks great.

---

## Step 5: Select a Voice

With your avatar selected, choose a voice from the voice panel. HeyGen offers:

- Multiple voice styles (conversational, energetic, calm, authoritative)
- 175+ languages and accents
- Adjustable speed and pitch

Preview voices by clicking the play button next to each one. Pick the voice that fits your content's energy.

**Pro tip:** If you're creating content in English, try the "Conversational" style voices — they sound the most natural and less like a robot reading a teleprompter.

---

## Step 6: Customize the Background and Layout

HeyGen gives you several layout options:

- **Talking Head** — Avatar centered, shoulders up. Best for direct-to-camera content.
- **With Media** — Avatar in a corner, slides or images in the main area. Great for presentations and tutorials.
- **Full Screen** — Avatar fills the entire frame. Maximum presence.

You can also:
- Upload a custom background image
- Choose a solid color or gradient
- Add your logo as a watermark

For a product intro, try the **With Media** layout and upload a screenshot or product image.

---

## Step 7: Preview and Generate

Click **Preview** to see a short preview of your video. Check for:

- **Lip-sync accuracy** — Does the mouth movement match the words?
- **Pacing** — Any rushed or awkward pauses? Adjust your script punctuation.
- **Voice tone** — Does it match your brand?

When you're happy, click **Generate Video**. Processing takes 2–5 minutes depending on length.

Your video downloads as an MP4 in 1080p (free tier) or 4K (Creator plan).

## Step 8: Translate Your Video (Optional — Killer Feature)

Here's where HeyGen gets powerful for solopreneurs. After creating your English video, you can translate it into any of 175+ languages:

1. Open your finished video
2. Click **Translate**
3. Select target language(s)
4. HeyGen clones your voice and re-syncs lip movements to match the new language

You now have the same video in Spanish, French, German, Japanese — whatever markets you serve. This is genuinely game-changing for freelancers with international clients or course creators who want global reach.
