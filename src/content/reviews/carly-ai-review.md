## What Is Carly AI?

Carly AI is an AI scheduling assistant that acts like an executive assistant for meeting coordination. Unlike Calendly or scheduling links, Carly works inside your existing email and text conversations. You CC Carly on an email thread, forward a messy conversation, or send a screenshot, and it handles the scheduling back-and-forth autonomously.

It integrates with Google Calendar, Outlook, Zoom, Teams, and Meet, and offers a Gmail Chrome extension for quick access.

## The Good

### Works Where You Already Communicate
Carly's biggest advantage is zero context switching. You don't ask clients to click a booking link; you simply CC carly@usecarly.com on an email, and it takes over. It also works via SMS and WhatsApp, making it ideal for clients who prefer texting.

### Understands Complex Email Threads
In testing, Carly correctly parsed scheduling requests from long email chains with multiple participants, quoted messages, and even screenshots of calendars. It proposed times that worked for everyone and sent invites automatically.

### Learns Your Preferences
Over two weeks of use, Carly learned to block my 9–11 AM focus time, avoid scheduling before 8 AM, and prefer Zoom over Teams for client calls. It started suggesting times that matched my habits without me having to specify each time.

### AI-Native CRM (Personal Plan)
The Personal plan includes a lightweight CRM that logs contacts from scheduling interactions, notes meeting outcomes, and sets follow-up reminders. It's not HubSpot, but it's useful for tracking who you've met with.

### Transparent and Affordable
At $19/mo annually (or $22/mo month-to-month), Carly is priced competitively with tools like Motion ($19/mo) and Reclaim ($10/mo), but offers more advanced conversational abilities.

---

## The Bad

### Requires Calendar Access
Carly needs read/write access to your Google or Outlook calendar. If you're uncomfortable granting that to a third-party AI, this is a non-starter.

### Not an Inbox Manager
While Carly handles scheduling emails, it doesn't help with general email triage, drafting non-scheduling replies, or inbox zero. For that, you'd still need Superhuman, Shortwave, or an AI email assistant like Lindy.

### Web Chat Limited on Free Plan
The Free plan only works via web chat at usecarly.com. To get email/SMS integration, you need the Personal plan. There's no free trial of the paid features.

### Occasional Misinterpretations
In about 5% of test cases, Carly misread a time zone or suggested a time outside my working hours when I hadn't explicitly blocked it. A quick correction email fixed it, but it requires oversight.

## Who Should Use Carly AI

**Carly is worth it if:**
- You spend 3+ hours per week on scheduling back-and-forth
- Your clients prefer email or text over booking links
- You want an assistant that learns your scheduling habits over time
- You need SMS/WhatsApp scheduling capabilities
- $19/mo is <5% of your monthly revenue

**Carly isn't worth it if:**
- You rarely schedule meetings (<3 per week)
- You're uncomfortable granting calendar access to a third party
- You primarily need link-based scheduling (Calendly is simpler)
- You want an all-in-one email assistant (try Lindy or Superhuman instead)
