**Quick verdict:** Notion is the closest thing to a complete operating system for a one-person business. The AI add-on makes it even more powerful. If you're juggling multiple tools for docs, tasks, notes, and project management — switch to Notion.

---

## What Is Notion?

Notion is an all-in-one workspace that combines documents, project management, databases, wikis, and calendars in a single tool. Think Google Docs + Trello + Evernote + Airtable merged into one.

The AI add-on ($10/mo) adds smart features: content generation, summaries, action item extraction, translation, and autofill for databases.

## The Good

### One Tool Replaces Five

Before Notion: Google Docs + Trello + Evernote + Google Sheets + a wiki. Now it's just Notion. Everything connects — client projects link to meeting notes, which link to action items, which link to your content calendar.

### AI Summaries Are Excellent

Paste a 2,000-word meeting transcript, select "Summarize," and get a clean summary with action items in seconds. Saves 20-30 minutes per client meeting.

### Databases Are Incredibly Flexible

Notion databases aren't simple spreadsheets. They're relational databases with multiple views (table, board, calendar, timeline, gallery). Create client CRMs, content calendars, project trackers, invoice systems — all connected.

### Free Plan Is Generous

Most solopreneurs can run their entire business on the free plan. Unlimited pages, all block types, API access.

---

## The Bad

### Steep Learning Curve

Expect to spend 1-2 weeks learning pages, blocks, databases, relations, rollups, formulas, views. It's not intuitive at first. Start with a template.

### AI Add-on Feels Overpriced

$10/mo for AI features on top of your plan feels steep. Most AI features are available for free in ChatGPT. You're paying for convenience.

### Can Feel Slow

Large databases (500+ entries) load slowly. Pages with many embeds can lag.

### Mobile App Is Mediocre

Works but not great. Slow loading, limited database editing.

## Who Should Use Notion

**Perfect if:**
- You're juggling multiple tools
- You want docs, tasks, notes, and databases in one place
- You're willing to invest time in setup (1-2 weeks)
- You value flexibility over simplicity

**Not right if:**
- You need offline reliability
- You primarily need project management (Asana/ClickUp are simpler)
- You want something that works instantly without setup
